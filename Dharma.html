What is Dharma?

Dharma does not mean a riligion. riligion means a group, path or way of life. Dharma is the cosmic law that applies to every particle of the universe. Dharma is science; dharma is the law for matter and living beings. Dharma is the wave that creates and also destroys the creation.

What is Dharmachakra?

Dharmachakra is the dynamic form of that cosmic law, which continuously flows throughout the entire creation. Just as dharma is the law applicable to every particle of the universe, similarly dharmachakra symbolizes the cyclic motion of that law. Dharmachakra is not static; it represents the continuous cycle of creation, preservation, and transformation. It is the system under which matter originates, changes form, and merges again. Dharmachakra, from a scientific perspective, indicates the continuous operation of natural laws, and from a philosophical perspective, it symbolizes the balanced and just system of existence. Thus, dharmachakra is the perpetual motion of that eternal law, which sustains creation and also propels it forward through change.

What is the rule of following Dharma?

The rule of following dharma is not to follow the traditions of a particular riligion, but to act in accordance with that cosmic law which is operative in every particle of creation. When thoughts, words, and deeds are in accordance with the truth, balance, and justice of nature, then dharma is followed. Following dharma means understanding the nature of matter, respecting the laws of life, and performing actions that maintain the equilibrium and balance of creation. Ignorance, excess, and imbalance are against dharma, while truth, discernment, and balance are the foundations of following dharma. Therefore, following dharma is that conscious state in which a person aligns their conduct with the cosmic laws.
